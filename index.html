For the One Who Finds This

Love meets you in the ordinary—
in chipped mug mornings, in the hallway light,
in the hush between your footsteps
when no one else is near.

It hides in the folds of laundry,
in the wind that brushes your sleeve,
in the stars that blink once,
as if remembering your name.

Love winds around you unseen—
a thread pulled soft through silence,
a melody too ancient to forget,
played low against the ribs of your heart.

A voice,
calling from the deep.
It’s from the One who is
walking near the walls of you,
gently gathering all the parts of you
that never knew they needed a song.

It does not shout.
It never forces.
But it stays.

It stays
in the breath between sorrows,
in the ache that almost breaks you—
and sings,
softly sings,
until you remember
you are not alone.
